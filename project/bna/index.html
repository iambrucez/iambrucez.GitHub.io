Blockchain Network Analysis: A Comparative Studies of Decentralized Banks

November 25, 2022

Authors: Yufan Zhang, Zichao Chen, Yutong Sun, Yulin Liu, Luyao Zhang

Paper accepted by SAI Computing Conference

GitHub

Abstract#

Decentralized finance (DeFi) is known for its unique mechanism design, which applies smart contracts to facilitate peer-to-peer transactions. The decentralized bank is a typical application of DeFi. Ideally, a decentralized bank should be decentralized in the transaction. However, many recent studies have found that decentralized bank has not achieved a significant degree of decentralization. This research conducts a parallel comparison study among mainstream decentralized banks. We apply core-periphery network features analysis using the transaction data from four decentralized banks, Liquity, Aave, MakerDao, and Compound. We extract six features of the transaction data and compare the banks’ levels of decentralization cross-sectionally. According to the analysis result, we find that: 1) Although decentralized banking transactions are supposed to be decentralized, the data shows that four banks have primary external transaction core addresses such as Huobi, Coinbase, Binance, etc. 2) MakerDao and Compound are more decentralized in the transaction than Aave and Liquity. In conclusion, we analyze the potential reasons that lead to different results of transaction network features. Our research reveals the feasibility of the comparison study on decentralized banks in the facet of transactions, identifies potential reasons that affect network decentralization and provides inspiration for future research and mechanism design on decentralized banks.
